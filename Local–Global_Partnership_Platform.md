# Idea 1: Local–Global Partnership Platform

## 1. Title
Local–Global Partnership Model for Iranian Tech Companies

## 2. Author
Sareh Ameri, PhD candidate
Mohammad Norouzi, PhD

## 3. Abstract
This idea proposes transforming major Iranian platforms (e.g., ride-hailing, e-commerce) into strategic partners of global companies instead of direct competitors. By enabling joint ventures or partial acquisitions, local firms can retain market share while benefiting from foreign technology, capital, and expertise.

## 4. Why It’s Interesting
With potential market liberalization, global players will likely enter Iran. Without preparation, local companies may collapse, causing job loss and shareholder damage. This model allows:
- Preservation of local employment
- Protection of shareholder value
- Faster modernization of services

## 5. Approach
- Phase 1 (0–6 months): Identify compatible global partners
- Phase 2 (6–12 months): Negotiate joint ventures or equity sales
- Phase 3 (1–2 years): Integrate systems, branding, and operations
- Legal framework for foreign ownership and partnerships

## 6. Help Needed
- Legal experts in international business
- Investment banks
- Government regulatory support
- Access to company financial data

## 7. Supporting Material
- Case studies: Grab–Uber deal
- Sample JV structures
- Market entry strategy charts

# Idea 2: Super App Transformation Strategy

## 1. Title
Building Iranian Super Apps for Competitive Advantage

## 2. Author
Sareh Ameri, PhD candidate
Mohammad Norouzi, PhD

## 3. Abstract
Convert existing platforms into “super apps” offering multiple services (transport, payments, shopping, finance). This increases user retention and creates a strong ecosystem that is harder for foreign competitors to replace.

## 4. Why It’s Interesting
Single-service apps are vulnerable to global competition. Super apps create:
- High switching costs
- Strong network effects
- Increased revenue streams

## 5. Approach
- Expand services into fintech, logistics, and daily services
- Integrate a unified wallet/payment system
- Use data to personalize services
- Timeline: 1–3 years gradual rollout

## 6. Help Needed
- Product managers
- AI/data teams
- Payment infrastructure
- Strategic investors

## 7. Supporting Material
- Super app ecosystem diagrams
- Revenue model comparisons
- User journey maps

# Idea 3: Market Stabilization Fund for Shareholders

## 1. Title
Equity Stabilization Fund for Tech Companies

## 2. Author
Sareh Ameri, PhD candidate
Mohammad Norouzi, PhD

## 3. Abstract
Create a financial safety mechanism to protect public shareholders from sudden losses due to market opening and foreign competition.

## 4. Why It’s Interesting
Millions of retail investors could face heavy losses if major companies collapse. This could:
- Damage trust in capital markets
- Trigger economic instability

## 5. Approach
- Establish a semi-public investment fund
- Buy shares during high volatility
- Support restructuring or mergers
- Timeline: Immediate setup before market opening

## 6. Help Needed
- Government backing
- Financial institutions
- Market analysts
- Regulatory approval

## 7. Supporting Material
- Financial risk models
- Historical examples from emerging markets
- Fund structure diagrams

# Idea 4: Regional Expansion Strategy

## 1. Title
Exporting Iranian Platforms to Regional Markets

## 2. Author
Sareh Ameri, PhD candidate
Mohammad Norouzi, PhD

## 3. Abstract
Encourage Iranian companies to expand into neighboring countries (Iraq, Central Asia, افغانستان) to diversify revenue and reduce dependency on domestic competition.

## 4. Why It’s Interesting
Local companies may struggle domestically but can compete effectively in culturally similar, less competitive markets.

## 5. Approach

- Localize services (language, payment systems)
- Partner with local businesses
- Timeline: 1–2 years per market

- Localization teams
- Cross-border payment solutions
- Logistics partners

# Idea 6: Controlled Market Entry Policy

## 1. Title  
Phased Entry Framework for Foreign Competitors

## 2. Author  
Sareh Ameri, PhD candidate
Mohammad Norouzi, PhD

## 3. Abstract  
Introduce foreign competitors gradually through regulation, allowing domestic companies time to adapt instead of facing immediate full-scale competition.

## 4. Why It’s Interesting  
Sudden market liberalization can damage entire industries. A phased approach:  
- Reduces economic shock  
- Protects employment  
- Encourages fair and sustainable competition  

## 5. Approach  
- Year 1: Market share limits for foreign companies  
- Years 2–3: Gradual reduction of restrictions  
- Require partnerships with domestic firms  
- Monitor and enforce fair competition  

## 6. Help Needed  
- Policymakers  
- Economic advisors  
- Legal framework designers  

## 7. Supporting Material  
- Policy models from the EU and Asia  
- Economic impact simulations  
- Regulatory flowcharts
