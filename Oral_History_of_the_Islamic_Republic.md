## Abstract  
This project proposes a comprehensive oral history initiative documenting the lived experiences of officials who served within the Islamic Republic of Iran. Modeled in part on efforts like the Iran Oral History Project (Harvard University initiative), which captured perspectives from officials of the Pahlavi dynasty, this initiative would focus on individuals across different levels of governance within the Islamic Republic.

The goal is to systematically record firsthand accounts of decision-making processes, institutional dynamics, and the role of ideology and religion in governance. These testimonies would provide a valuable historical record for researchers, policymakers, and future generations seeking to understand how the system functioned internally.

---

## Why It’s Interesting  
- **Historical urgency**: Firsthand accounts are perishable; capturing them early preserves institutional memory.  
- **Comparative value**: Enables future comparison between pre- and post-revolution governance systems.  
- **Insight into decision-making**: Reveals how policy was actually formed versus how it was presented publicly.  
- **Understanding ideology in practice**: Examines how Islamic principles influenced (or conflicted with) administrative and political decisions.  
- **Post-transition relevance**: If political change occurs, such archives become foundational for accountability, scholarship, and reconciliation.

## Approach  

### 1. Scoping & Framework (0–3 months)  
- Define target groups: ministers, mid-level bureaucrats, advisors, military-linked administrators, etc.  
- Develop standardized interview protocols (themes: decision-making, hierarchy, religion, crisis moments).  

### 2. Pilot Interviews (3–6 months)  
- Conduct a small number of in-depth interviews to refine methodology.  
- Test formats: video, audio, transcript-first.  

### 3. Full Collection Phase (6–24 months)  
- Record long-form interviews (2–10 hours per subject).  
- Prioritize diversity of roles and viewpoints.  

### 4. Archiving & Access  
- Build a secure, indexed digital archive.  
- Tag by topic (e.g., economic policy, foreign relations, religious authority).  

### 5. Publication & Dissemination  
- Edited volumes, documentaries, and research papers.  
- Controlled public access depending on sensitivity.
