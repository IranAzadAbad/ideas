## Abstract

A platform connecting Iranian diaspora software engineers (in the US, EU, Asia) with early-stage tech companies, startups, and scale-ups building in Iran. Solves two problems: companies struggling to find experienced engineers, and diaspora talent wanting to contribute without relocating or taking on currency/banking risks.

This isn't outsourcing—it's enabling remote work partnerships where diaspora engineers can join Iranian teams part-time or full-time with proper legal structures and stable payment methods.

---

## Why It's Interesting

**Why now**:
- Iran's tech ecosystem is growing (1000+ startups estimated) but faces a talent bottleneck
- Remote work is now normalized post-pandemic
- Sanctions create banking friction that a trusted platform can help navigate
- ~1.5M Iranian diaspora in tech hubs globally with desire to contribute

**Why Iran**:
- Brain drain is real: top talent often leaves for safety/opportunity and doesn't return
- This creates a way for diaspora talent to stay connected and invested
- Iranian startups are capital-constrained but human-capital constrained even more
- Unique regulatory challenges around payments and contracts require Iran-specific expertise

**Expected impact**:
- Year 1: 50-100 diaspora engineers on platform, 15-20 active matches
- Year 2: 300+ engineers, 60+ companies using it, ~$50K-100K in monthly payroll flowing through platform
- Year 3: Become the de facto way diaspora engineers contribute; possible expansion to other roles (design, product, operations)

## Approach

### Key Steps

1. **MVP (Months 1-3)**: Build basic platform for profiles and matching
   - Simple engineer profiles (skills, availability, timezone)
   - Company profiles and job postings
   - Direct messaging for initial conversations
   - Research: interview 20 diaspora engineers and 10 companies about pain points

2. **Legal & Payment Layer (Months 3-6)**: Solve the hard problem
   - Partner with fintech for legal cross-border payments (or establish trusted escrow model)
   - Create template contracts for diaspora-Iran remote work
   - Compliance research: employment law, sanctions implications, tax
   - Get legal review from Iran-based and diaspora lawyers

3. **Community & Vetting (Months 6-9)**: Build trust
   - Verification: GitHub portfolios, references from mutual networks
   - Community guidelines for professional conduct
   - Success stories and case studies
   - Mentor matching for early-career diaspora engineers

4. **Scale & Sustainability (Months 9+)**: Revenue model
   - Small platform fee or commission on successful matches
   - Premium features (verified profiles, priority matching)
   - Potential expansion to investors, mentors, advisors

### Timeline

MVP to basic viability: 9-12 months. Full sustainability: 18-24 months.

### Methods & Technology

- **Stack**: Web app (React/Vue frontend, Node or Python backend), basic matching algorithm
- **Process**: Build in public—share progress with both diaspora and Iran startup communities
- **Initial partnerships**: Reach out to existing networks (Alumni associations, tech Telegram channels, startup hubs like Tehran's IITA)

### Go-to-Market

- **Phase 1**: Recruit diaspora engineers first (easier, global reach). Then approach Iranian tech companies.
- **Phase 2**: Partner with accelerators and startup hubs (e.g., Khwarizmi Startups) for company recruitment
- **Phase 3**: Word-of-mouth and case studies (diaspora will refer friends if it works)

## Help Needed

- **Full-stack engineer(s)**: 1-2 people, 6-12 months to build MVP. Prefer someone with fintech or marketplace experience.

- **Community manager**: 1 person (post-MVP) to recruit diaspora engineers, manage community, gather feedback.
- **Product/designer**: Part-time help for user research and interface design.
- **Funding**: ~$40-80K for 6 months (salaries, legal fees, research travel to Iran for interviews).
- **Partnerships**: Introductions to accelerators, startup hubs, diaspora networks (Telegram, Discord, alumni groups).

## Notes

- **Assumption**: That diaspora engineers want to contribute but need a trusted platform + legal clarity. Validate this with interviews.
- **Risk**: Payment solution is the hardest part. If banking/payments can't be solved, the idea fails. Recommend exploring this first.
- **Regulatory uncertainty**: Sanctions and foreign exchange laws are complex. Suggest getting legal clarity early, even if conservative.
